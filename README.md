# EcoQuest: Campus Adventure

## Overview
EcoQuest: Campus Adventure is a gamified mobile platform that helps college students build eco-friendly habits through quick, rewarding daily actions. The concept blends food systems, energy efficiency, and circular economy behaviors into a playful campus experience inspired by platformer games like Super Mario, streak systems like Duolingo, and reward loops like Candy Crush.

Players complete short "eco quests" such as using leftovers, turning off unused appliances, sharing extra food, or taking shorter showers. Each completed action earns coins, XP, badges, streak bonuses, and progress toward campus rewards.

## Problem Statement
College students living in dorms, apartments, and shared housing often struggle to maintain sustainable habits because their routines are busy, inconsistent, and budget-driven.

Key challenges:
- Food waste from overbuying, poor storage, missed meals, and leftover neglect
- Energy inefficiency from lights left on, overused mini-fridges, microwaves, and shared appliances
- Low engagement because sustainability tools often feel inconvenient, boring, or disconnected from daily student life

Why this matters:
- Small wasteful habits scale quickly across residence halls and student apartments
- Students lose money through wasted groceries and unnecessary utility use
- Campuses miss a major opportunity to build long-term sustainability culture through habit formation

## Target User
Primary audience:
- College students living on or near campus
- Dorm residents, apartment renters, and students in shared housing

User traits:
- Busy and schedule-driven
- Budget-conscious
- Highly mobile-first and tech-comfortable
- Motivated by competition, social recognition, rewards, and visible progress

## Solution
EcoQuest turns sustainable living into a campus adventure game.

Instead of asking students to completely change their lifestyle, the app breaks sustainability into small, low-friction daily actions they can already do. Each action becomes a level, challenge, or collectible moment in the game.

Core idea:
- Real-world eco actions become game quests
- Progress is visual, social, and rewarding
- Individual habits contribute to dorm-wide and campus-wide impact

## Core Features

### 1. Daily Eco Levels
Each day, players receive short sustainability tasks that fit student life.

Example quests:
- Cook with leftovers: `+10 coins`
- Turn off lights and appliances before leaving: `+5 coins`
- Share or donate extra food before it spoils: `+10 coins`
- Take a shorter shower: `+5 coins`
- Use a reusable bottle or container: `+5 coins`

These appear as daily levels on a campus map so students feel like they are progressing through an adventure, not checking items off a boring list.

### 2. Coins, XP, and Avatars
Players earn:
- Coins for task completion
- XP for consistent activity
- Avatar upgrades, badges, cosmetics, and titles for leveling up

This creates immediate satisfaction while also supporting long-term retention.

### 3. Streaks and Power-Ups
Daily streaks unlock gameplay boosts such as:
- Double coin days
- Bonus chests
- Special event access
- Cosmetic power-ups for avatars

This encourages consistent behavior without requiring expensive incentives.

### 4. Enemies and Boss Battles
Negative habits become playful obstacles:
- Food Waste Monster: defeated by sharing leftovers or using food before it expires
- Energy Goblin: defeated by reducing unnecessary energy use
- Water Waster: defeated by shortening showers or reporting leaks

Group boss battles can turn entire dorms or student organizations into teams working toward shared goals such as:
- Save a target amount of energy
- Divert food waste from trash
- Complete a campus compost challenge

### 5. Leaderboards and Social Motivation
Players can compete with:
- Friends
- Roommates
- Dorm floors
- Student organizations

This adds friendly competition, campus identity, and peer accountability.

### 6. Impact Dashboard
The app shows visible outcomes from small actions, including:
- Food saved
- Energy saved
- Water conserved
- CO2 emissions reduced

Students do not just earn points. They see proof that their habits matter.

### 7. Rewards and Campus Partnerships
The MVP can work with virtual rewards only, but the model becomes stronger with local partners.

Potential incentives:
- Dining discounts
- Loyalty program integration
- Rental or equipment discounts
- Early access to campus perks
- Freebies tied to milestone achievements

Possible partners:
- Campus dining programs
- Local restaurants
- Student housing offices
- UNC Charlotte loyalty and student engagement programs

## Gameplay Inspiration
The experience borrows the emotional hooks of familiar games and maps them to sustainability behavior.

Game inspiration:
- Super Mario: levels, coins, power-ups, enemies, boss fights, exploration
- Duolingo: streaks, daily goals, low-friction habit loops
- Candy Crush: satisfying progression, reward bursts, clear milestones

Example game translations:
- Coins = points earned from real eco actions
- Levels = daily quests or weekly challenge paths
- Power-ups = streak bonuses and reward multipliers
- Boss fights = group sustainability goals
- Side quests = optional bonus actions such as food donation or community cleanups

Possible tier benefits:

## Circular Economy Extension
EcoQuest can go beyond personal habits by connecting students to local food redistribution and reuse systems.

Future opportunities:
- Connect restaurants or dining halls with leftover food distribution
- Help students post surplus groceries before they expire
- Encourage reuse, borrowing, and sharing systems across campus housing
- Track community-level waste reduction over time

This strengthens the circular economy pillar by shifting from individual behavior tracking to community resource flow.

## Behavior Change Strategy
EcoQuest is designed around behavior change principles that fit student life.

How it changes behavior:
- Gamification: makes sustainability feel fun and rewarding
- Immediate feedback: shows progress and environmental impact right away
- Social proof: uses friends, dorms, and teams to motivate action
- Low friction: focuses on quick, realistic actions
- Reward loops: encourages repetition through streaks, tiers, and unlockables

Why awareness alone fails:
- Students already know sustainability matters
- The real barrier is low motivation, low visibility, and low convenience
- EcoQuest closes that gap by making the desired behavior easier and more engaging
